Exercise Routines

Wrist Warm-Up Routine
Lower Back Strength Routine
Exercise Description Reps/Sets
Jumping Jacks/Light Jogging General warm-up to increase blood flow and warm up the body. 1 minute (continuous)
Arm Circles Loosens shoulders and arms, improving range of motion. 30 seconds forward, 30 seconds backward
Wrist Rotations Gently rotates wrists in both directions to improve mobility. 15 seconds each direction
Wrist Flexor Stretch Stretches the underside of the forearm to relieve tension in wrist flexors. 30 seconds per arm (1 set)
Wrist Extensor Stretch Stretches the top side of the forearm to target wrist extensors. 30 seconds per arm (1 set)
Finger Tendon Glides Mobilizes finger tendons by opening and closing fists. 10 reps (1 set)
Finger Flicks Activates finger muscles by rapidly opening and closing hands. 20–30 seconds
Forearm Rotations (Supination/Pronation) Strengthens forearm muscles by rotating palm up and down. 10–15 reps per arm (1 set)
Wall Shoulder Stretch Stretches shoulders and chest to prepare for upper body activity. 30 seconds (1 set)
Light Dynamic Hang (optional) Light hanging to activate grip and forearm muscles. Hold 5–10 seconds (1 set)
Exercise Description Reps/Sets
Cat-Cow Stretch Mobilizes the spine, improving flexibility and relieving tightness. 1 minute (continuous)
Bird-Dog Strengthens the lower back and core by extending opposite arms and legs. 10 reps per side (1 set)
Glute Bridge Strengthens glutes and lower back, promoting pelvic stability. 10–15 reps (1 set)
Plank Full-body core stabilization to support the lower back. Hold for 30–60 seconds (1 set)
Superman Targets lower back muscles by lifting arms, chest, and legs off the floor simultaneously. 10 reps (1 set)
Child’s Pose Gently stretches the lower back and hips, relieving tension. Hold for 30–60 seconds
Pelvic Tilts Activates the lower back and core, improving spinal flexibility. 10 reps (1 set)
